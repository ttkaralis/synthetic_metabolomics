# **Synthesizing metabolomics with generative artificial intelligence**

# **Introduction**

Over recent decades, omics technologies have undergone substantial advances, leading to the accumulation of large-scale “big data” that can be mined to uncover novel biological insights. However, metabolomics still lags behind other omics disciplines, primarily due to technical challenges in metabolite extraction and identification. As a result, metabolic research would greatly benefit from the availability of large, well-characterized metabolomic datasets. Here, we describe the generation of a large metabolomic dataset (10,000 samples and 218 metabolites) using generative artificial intelligence. Through extensive benchmarking of the synthetic data, we show that the resulting dataset exhibits high fidelity. Moreover, the generative AI model in this work can be used to synthesize virtually unlimited numbers of additional samples. Together, the synthetic metabolomic dataset and its generator enable the simulation of large-scale metabolomic data for diverse downstream applications, thereby supporting and accelerating metabolic research.
